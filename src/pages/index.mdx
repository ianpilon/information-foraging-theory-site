# Index

Wiki on information foraging theory and Peter Pirolli's broader research program on computational models of human-information interaction at three timescales.

## Sources

- [source-pirolli-nytech-2016](/source-pirolli-nytech-2016): NYTECH talk transcript, Oct 25 2016. The single source for this wiki as of 2026-05-20.
- [source-the-bloodhound-project](/source-the-bloodhound-project): The Bloodhound Project- Automating Discovery of Web Usability Issues using the InfoScent™ Simulator.pdf

## Foundational frame

- [information-foraging-theory](/information-foraging-theory): humans search for information the way animals forage for food.
- [optimal-foraging-theory](/optimal-foraging-theory): the biological theory the analogy was borrowed from.
- [timescales-of-human-behavior](/timescales-of-human-behavior): Newell's framing that different mechanisms dominate at different time orders of magnitude.
- [peter-pirolli](/peter-pirolli): cognitive scientist, originator of the theory, speaker of the source talk.

## Foraging mechanisms (seconds to minutes)

- [information-scent](/information-scent): local cues that drive within-page navigation.
- [information-patch-foraging](/information-patch-foraging): stay-or-leave decisions across pages and sites.
- [spreading-activation](/spreading-activation): the associative memory mechanism behind relevance judgments.
- [information-scent-absorption-rate](/information-scent-absorption-rate): a method to measure the navigability of a site
- [web-user-flow-by-information-scent](/web-user-flow-by-information-scent): a predictive simulation technique

## Foraging modeling stack

- [act-r](/act-r): cognitive architecture and simulation system that hosts the models.
- [snif-act](/snif-act): the foraging simulator built on ACT-R, predicts 70 to 80 percent of user navigation variability.
- [rational-analysis](/rational-analysis): the methodology that pairs environment modeling with cognitive modeling.

## Sense-making (minutes to hours)

- [sense-making](/sense-making): ill-structured tasks where users build mental models from large amounts of data.
- [topic-modeling-knowledge-tracing](/topic-modeling-knowledge-tracing): inferring user knowledge gain from what they read.
- [credibility-judgments](/credibility-judgments): how people decide which sources to trust on social media.
- [twitter-expert-ranking](/twitter-expert-ranking): applied algorithm built on credibility work.

## Behavior change (months to years)

- [behavior-change](/behavior-change): long-term lifestyle and habit change.
- [habit-formation](/habit-formation): two-circuit neuroscience and the goal-striving to habit-loop transition.
- [self-efficacy](/self-efficacy): Bandura's concept, modeled computationally as impulse-decay in ACT-R.
- [guided-inactive-mastery](/guided-inactive-mastery): personalization technique that produced 65 to 70 percent compliance.
- [fittle](/fittle): smartphone app that operationalizes the above.

## Applied UX outputs

- [information-foraging-tools](/information-foraging-tools): Bloodhound, CogTool, hyperbolic browser, table lens, scent trails, relevance-enhanced thumbnails.

## Forward-looking themes

- [trust-in-autonomous-systems](/trust-in-autonomous-systems): the closing theme. As AI and autonomous systems pervade everyday life, the credibility-judgment machinery built for social media gets reused.
