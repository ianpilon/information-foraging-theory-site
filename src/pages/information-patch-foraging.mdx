# Information Patch Foraging

**Summary**: The continuous decision a user makes about whether to keep looking on the current page or site versus giving up and going elsewhere. Borrowed from the animal foraging decision of when to leave a depleted food patch.

**Sources**: NYTECH- Models of Human-Information Interaction for UX Design 10-25-16.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-20

---

## The decision

While following [information-scent](/information-scent) within a site, the user is also continuously asking: is the stuff I am looking for here, or do I need to give up and search somewhere else? This is the patch decision, and people make it constantly without realizing it.

## The empirical pattern

When Pirolli's group plotted the distribution of "path lengths people pursued at a particular website before they gave up" on log-log axes, they observed an almost-straight line with a small leg. That shape is an inverse Gaussian distribution. The [snif-act](/snif-act) simulator predicts this distribution accurately for a given task and website, providing evidence that the underlying patch-leaving mechanism is being captured.

## Why this matters

For UX, patch foraging tells you that:

- Users do not exhaust a site before leaving; they apply an ongoing cost-benefit calculation
- The right design question is not "what is on this page" but "is the scent strong enough to justify another step"
- Drop-off is not a sign of failure to find; it is often a rational decision to switch patches
- A site can be optimized by lowering the cost of evaluating whether to continue, not only by making the answer easier to find

## How it pairs with scent

[information-scent](/information-scent) governs the within-patch navigation (which link to click). Patch foraging governs the between-patch decision (when to leave). The two together describe most of what a foraging user does.
