# Timescales of Human Behavior

**Summary**: Alan Newell's framing that human behavior operates at distinct timescales, and that different mechanisms and models are appropriate at each. Pirolli uses this as the organizing principle for his research program.

**Sources**: NYTECH- Models of Human-Information Interaction for UX Design 10-25-16.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-20

---

## The bands

| Band | Order of magnitude | Dominant mechanism | What you model |
|---|---|---|---|
| Biological | 10 to 100 ms | Neural machinery | Neural spike timing, neural information processing |
| Cognitive | 100 ms to seconds | Information processing operations | Memory retrieval, relevance judgment, decision |
| Rational | Minutes to hours | Goal-directed cost-benefit reasoning | Task organization, navigation through information spaces |
| Social | Days to years | Interaction with other people, culture | Communication, social phenomena |

The "rational band of analysis" is the level at which [information-foraging-theory](/information-foraging-theory) operates. People navigate cost-benefit landscapes; you can predict their choices by asking what an ideal rational agent would do.

## Why this matters for modeling

The kinds of models you build are different at each level:

- At the millisecond level you worry about neural circuits
- At the second level you worry about modules like memory, perception, and motor control (the territory of [act-r](/act-r))
- At the hour level you worry about how information environments are architected
- At the year level you worry about habit formation, social influence, and culture

A model pitched at the wrong level will miss the dominant phenomenon. A "rational band" model cannot explain reaction times. A neural model cannot explain why someone abandoned a website.

## How Pirolli uses this

The 2016 talk covers three of his research programs, each pitched at a different timescale:

- Seconds to minutes: [information-foraging-theory](/information-foraging-theory) (navigation, browsing, relevance)
- Minutes to hours: [sense-making](/sense-making) (organizing information into mental models)
- Months to years: [behavior-change](/behavior-change) (habit formation, lifestyle change)
