# Peter Pirolli

**Summary**: Cognitive scientist, fellow at Xerox PARC, originator of [information-foraging-theory](/information-foraging-theory). Speaker of the 2016 NYTECH talk that this wiki is built from.

**Sources**: NYTECH- Models of Human-Information Interaction for UX Design 10-25-16.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-20

---

## Background

- PhD in psychology from Carnegie Mellon University
- Tenured professor at UC Berkeley before joining Xerox PARC
- Fellow and manager of the Interactive Intelligence Area at Xerox PARC at time of talk (2016)
- Fellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science

## Intellectual lineage

Pirolli situates himself as part of a tradition that traces back to:

- **Doug Engelbart**, inventor of the mouse and giver of the "mother of all demos" in 1968, who argued that personal computing could augment human intelligence
- **Alan Newell**, father of artificial intelligence and cognitive science, who worked with Herbert Simon at CMU to understand how the mind exists in the physical world. Newell's idea of [timescales-of-human-behavior](/timescales-of-human-behavior) is the organizing frame Pirolli still uses
- **Stuart Card and Tom Moran**, Pirolli's longtime PARC collaborators, who with Newell wrote *The Psychology of Human-Computer Interaction* in the early 1980s, the first systematic synthesis of cognitive psychology applied to interactive systems

His own stated interest: "to deeply understand and model human cognition in a way that could be applied to these kinds of systems. To leverage that to engineer and design new kinds of systems to make people smarter, better, healthier."

## Research thread (covered in talk)

Three research programs at different [timescales-of-human-behavior](/timescales-of-human-behavior):

1. [information-foraging-theory](/information-foraging-theory) (seconds to minutes), with [snif-act](/snif-act) as the technical artifact
2. [sense-making](/sense-making) (minutes to hours), with [topic-modeling-knowledge-tracing](/topic-modeling-knowledge-tracing) and [credibility-judgments](/credibility-judgments) as recent extensions
3. [behavior-change](/behavior-change) (months to years), with [fittle](/fittle) as the deployment

## Note on the source

The PDF that this wiki is built from is a raw speech-to-text transcript. The OCR misspells Pirolli as "Paroli" throughout. Collaborator names are similarly garbled (Wai-Tat Fu becomes "Tatfou," Ramana Rao becomes "Ramna Rao," Vera Liao becomes "Vera Laugh"). The wiki uses the corrected names where they have been identified with confidence.
