# Optimal Foraging Theory

**Summary**: A branch of mathematical biology that analyzes how organisms search for and consume resources in their environment, evaluating whether their behaviors are well tuned to maximize energy intake and reproductive success.

**Sources**: NYTECH- Models of Human-Information Interaction for UX Design 10-25-16.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-20

---

## The biological theory

Optimal foraging theory is applied mathematics: given an environment and an organism's set of behaviors for finding and consuming things, how well engineered are those behaviors for getting energy out of the environment? The empirical finding across many species: most animals are very well designed for finding and consuming energy in their environments.

## The analogy to information

Pirolli and Card mapped the framework onto information work:

- The virtual world (the web, document collections, visual displays) is populated with useful information instead of food
- Technology provides a way to navigate through that world
- A user is a forager trying to maximize useful information gained per unit of interaction time

This analogy produced [information-foraging-theory](/information-foraging-theory).

## Two sides of the same problem

The theory looks at two complementary questions:

1. How do people shape themselves to their information environment so they get the most out of it?
2. How do you shape the information environment so it can be better foraged?

The first is descriptive (model the user). The second is prescriptive (design the system). They are coupled: better models of the user enable better design of the environment.
